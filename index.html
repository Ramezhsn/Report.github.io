Context analysis

➤ The recent escalation in conflict within Palestine has coincided with heightened tensions along Lebanon's southern borders, leading to confrontations within Lebanese territory. This escalation marks a departure from the relatively stable rules of engagement observed since approximately 2012. Observers draw parallels between the current tensions and the 2006 conflict between Lebanon and Israel, noting similarities in intensity and dynamics.

Assessing the impact on Lebanon, it's crucial to consider the potential consequences of conflict escalation, particularly regarding internal displacement and humanitarian needs. Understanding the evolving situation is essential for effective response planning and assistance delivery.

➤ The looming threat of an armed confrontation is alarming. Such conflict might see intense air raids and attacks, targeting not only Lebanon's southern regions but also the densely populated southern suburbs of Beirut, the agriculturally crucial northern Beqaa, and border areas. The escalation of the situation cannot be anticipated due to the dynamic and volatile nature of the situation.

➤ The assumed scenarios revolve around a nationwide scale. The 3 governorates that are at high risk as per historical data review are South Lebanon, Beqaa, and Beirut. The currently registered populace of these governorates is around 1,750,000, 51% Females and 49% Males, and out of the total number of people, 29% are children between the age of 0-18.

National Coordination and Preparedness

➤ The Lebanese government, led by the Prime Minister, has updated and published the national preparedness and emergency response plan to enhance response readiness. The humanitarian community, under the leadership of the Humanitarian Coordinator, is preparing a humanitarian contingency plan which will contribute to supplementing the efforts of the Government in case of conflict.

➤ Under the leadership of the Prime Minister, the Presidency of the Council of Ministers is managing the coordination of the national preparedness and response efforts, with the national Disaster Risk Management (DRM) Unit as technical support at national and sub-national level.

➤ At the sub-national level, Governors are leading on preparedness and emergency response in close coordination with the local authorities (Municipalities and Union of Municipalities) in line with the national Disaster Risk Reduction unit (DRR) strategy.

➤ The UN agencies and NGO partners continue to speed up advanced preparedness and response readiness efforts in support to the overall preparedness and response plan of the Government, including the immediate prepositioning of relief items and other commodities to cover priority needs in the first weeks of the crisis.

DRR Strategic Orientation

Goal: Safe and Disaster Resilient Communities in Lebanon

1 LRC Sectors

LRC sectors and external actors are efficiently working with a complementary approach to reduce community prioritized risks and vulnerabilities

2 Ministries & Governorates

Ministries and governorates conduct increased and more effective risk informed management and contingency planning

3 Local Authorities

Increased effectiveness in collective mobilization of available resources by local authorities and institutions to collaboratively engage in DRM

4 Vulnerable Groups

Improved ability of vulnerable groups and communities to cope with disasters and crises

DRR Interventions

Strengthen the resilience of communities in Lebanon and to make them safer through building the capacities of the communities and respective structures to prepare for and respond to looming threat

Implementation of Community Based Disaster Risk Management (CBDRM) approach that places community members at the heart of the interventions through participatory and inclusive programming during the assessment, design and implementation phases.
